Ubuntu 15.10

The first thing I was required to do to start this course clearly was to have a notebook.I'm sure no one would judge it a reason to be worried about but to me it represented the very first problem: recently I was using my brother's pc, since mine showed clear signs of death desire.
I've never been a geek, but neither I've been able to keep my notebook's safety at a decent level. So, thanks to Matteo Sirizzotti, a FabAcademy student of FabLab Toscana I resumed my 8-year old Asus F5Vseries, formatting it and installing Ubuntu 15.10 operating system. Then, I moved my first steps in using terminal commands and I installed Atom to start writing HTML codes.

Atom & HTML coding

I previously knew that approaching with the huge and unknown world of HTML wouldn't have been easy at all, but I found great help on w3schools.com This site helps in learning, testing, and training HTML code writing, by means of a long list of tutorials, divided by topics that also cointain areas dedicated to exercises.
The most efficient way of applying what I learned was to start from a prewritten html file and gradually change it, according to the commands I little by little discovered.
To do that, as I already said, I used Atom : it is a approachable text editor that you can easily use without ever touching a config file. Infact, it permits you to navigate easily among directories and files thanks to the menu in the left part of the page and it's also possibile to work on several files at the same time, thanks to tabs.

The first command I learned to use was " p " that allows you to divide the page body into paragraphs.
It represents the first success I had in HTML coding and that's why I feel fond of it. That's how you must type it in the text editor: 

	<p>
	Your text here
	</p>

Project Planning

As I said, at the beginning of the course I qas asked to put down some ideas about my final project. I wanted to make something concerning my main interests that could be useful at the same time, therefore I focused on nature and, particularly, on water need of plant.
So, I put together the problem of water waste and the common habit of domestic gardening and I aimed at inventing a system that everyone could easily have at home, so as so to improve the water issue on a large scale, starting from single persons's little care.

Owing to my project ambition, I had to plan the steps that would have lead me to my goal in a rational way and even if that would seem quite simple, I must admit it's not like that, since I still don't know definitely the items I'll have to create and how I'll do it. Therefore, having no ideas about time, procedures and difficulties, I had to find a method to face the process of my project realization.

I discovered that plans can be made by and follow paths that can be the opposite one from another. After achieving a brief knowledge about the main ones and their principles, I set up my work according to The Spiral Model . This planning method proceeds with evolving cycles that, one by one, enhance the starting prototype structure and functionality.
This picture clearly represents how the project grow as the process goes on. The characteristics I found more suitable with my work were:

  • definition of the objective
  • concepts of requirements and operations
  • testing of the prototype at each cycle
  • considerations and possible improvements of the prototype

Infact, doing so, I think I'll better understand my project weak spots and potentialities during its realization, without compromising its functionality.
